The energy transition is often discussed in terms of carbon emissions. At *The Climate Ledger*, we look at it in terms of order books and margin expansion.

If the US moves toward a federal mandate or increased subsidies for heat pumps in 2026, the narrative shifts from "environmental necessity" to "industrial deployment." Here is where the smart money is looking.

### 1. The Pure Plays (HVAC Giants)
The obvious beneficiaries are the manufacturers with established supply chains.
* **Carrier Global (CARR):** They have pivoted aggressively toward electric solutions. A mandate effectively acts as a government-backed sales channel for their premium units.
* **Daikin Industries:** The Japanese giant has massive penetration in global markets and is scaling US manufacturing.

### 2. The "Pick and Shovel" Play: Copper
You cannot install millions of heat pumps without millions of tons of copper piping and wiring.
* Heat pumps require significantly more copper than traditional gas furnaces.
* This creates a supply squeeze for copper miners like **Freeport-McMoRan (FCX)**. The commodity price itself may be the best way to play this trend, bypassing the execution risk of individual manufacturers.

Don't just watch the policy headlines. Watch the raw material orders. When Carrier starts locking in long-term copper contracts, the mandate is already priced in.
